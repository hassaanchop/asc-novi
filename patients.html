Parking

Free parking is available directly in front of and surrounding our building. We offer single-level, accessible entry with dedicated handicap-accessible spaces near the main entrance.

Driving Directions

From I-96 Eastbound or Westbound:

Take exit 162 (Beck Road / Novi Road). Head north on Beck Road for approximately 1 mile. Turn right onto Providence Parkway. Our building is on the right at 26750 Providence Parkway, Suite 100.

From M-5 (Haggerty Road corridor):

Head east on 10 Mile Road. Turn left on Beck Road north, then right on Providence Parkway.

Bringing a Support Person

You are encouraged — and required for discharge — to have a responsible adult accompany you to your procedure. Your support person is welcome to wait in our comfortable family waiting area. We will keep them informed throughout your time with us.

For same-day procedures, your companion should plan to be available for 3–5 hours and to drive you home afterward.

Your Questions, Answered

Can't find what you're looking for? Call us at 248-662-1500 and we'll be happy to help.

An ambulatory surgery center (ASC) is a specialized outpatient facility where surgical procedures are performed and patients go home the same day. ASCs like ours offer many advantages over hospital-based surgery: a more personal, less rushed environment; typically shorter wait times; lower infection risk; and lower out-of-pocket costs for many patients. Our facility is fully accredited and equipped with the same advanced surgical technology found in hospital operating rooms.
You must have a responsible adult who is 18 or older drive you home after your procedure. Because of the effects of anesthesia and sedation, you cannot drive yourself or take a rideshare alone. We strongly recommend your support person stay with you for the first 24 hours at home. They are welcome to wait in our comfortable family area during your procedure.
Most patients are at our facility for 2–4 hours total, including pre-operative preparation, the procedure itself, and post-operative recovery. The exact duration depends on the type of procedure and how quickly you recover from anesthesia. Your clinical team will give you a more specific estimate during your pre-surgical phone call.
For non-urgent questions during business hours, call us at 248-662-1500. For urgent medical concerns after hours, contact your surgeon's answering service. If you believe you are experiencing a medical emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. Our team will address any last-minute questions first thing in the morning on your procedure day.
Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that is easy to put on and take off. For lower extremity procedures (hip, knee, ankle, foot), wear shorts or pants with loose legs. For upper extremity procedures (shoulder, elbow, wrist, hand), wear a short-sleeve or sleeveless top. Avoid tight clothing, complex fastenings, and jewelry. Leave valuables at home.
The type of anesthesia used depends on your procedure, your health history, and your surgeon's and anesthesiologist's recommendations. Options may include general anesthesia (fully asleep), regional anesthesia (numb from a specific area down), sedation (deeply relaxed but not fully asleep), or local anesthesia with monitored care. Your anesthesiologist will discuss your options with you before the procedure and will honor your preferences where medically appropriate.

Keep in mind that you may receive separate statements from our facility, your surgeon, and your anesthesiologist — these are normal and reflect different components of your care.
