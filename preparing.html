Preparing for Surgery

We know that preparing for a procedure can feel overwhelming. Our team is here to walk you through every step — so you arrive informed, prepared, and at ease.

Questions before your procedure? Call us anytime at 248-662-1500. Our clinical team is happy to help — no question is too small.

Step 1

Pre-Surgical Phone Call

Before your procedure, a member of our clinical nursing team will contact you by phone. This call is an important part of your care — please plan to be available for it, as it helps us tailor your experience and ensure your safety.

During this call, we will:

  • Review your complete medical and surgical history
  • Confirm your current medications and any allergies
  • Discuss anesthesia options and your preferences
  • Provide your specific arrival time and parking information
  • Explain what to expect on the day of surgery
  • Answer any questions you or your support person may have

Tip: Have a list of your current medications (including over-the-counter drugs and supplements) ready for this call. Being thorough here helps us provide the safest possible care.

Step 2

Pre-Surgical Instructions

Following these instructions carefully helps ensure your surgery goes smoothly and safely. Your clinical team will confirm the specifics during your pre-surgical call, as some instructions may vary based on your procedure and health history.

Eating & Drinking

  • Do not eat any solid food after midnight the night before your surgery
  • You may drink clear liquids (water, apple juice, black coffee, clear broth) up to 2 hours before your scheduled arrival time
  • Do not drink alcohol for at least 24 hours before your procedure
  • Do not chew gum or use tobacco products on the day of surgery

Medications

  • Take your regular morning medications with a small sip of water unless instructed otherwise
  • Blood thinners (warfarin, aspirin, Plavix, etc.) may need to be stopped — your surgeon's office will advise
  • Bring a complete list of all medications and dosages on the day of surgery
  • Do not take herbal supplements in the week before surgery without consulting your surgeon

What to Bring

  • A photo ID and insurance card(s)
  • Your co-pay or any outstanding balance (we accept major credit cards, checks, and cash)
  • Comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that is easy to put on and remove
  • Slip-on shoes — avoid laces if your procedure involves a lower extremity
  • A responsible adult who can drive you home and stay with you for the first 24 hours
  • Any splints, braces, or assistive devices you currently use

Day of Surgery

  • Shower and wash the surgical area thoroughly the night before or the morning of surgery
  • Remove all nail polish and artificial nails on the surgical side
  • Leave jewelry, watches, and valuables at home
  • Do not wear makeup, lotion, or deodorant on the day of surgery
  • Arrive at the time specified during your pre-surgical call — typically 1–2 hours before your procedure

Important: If you develop a cold, fever, infection, or any sudden health change in the days before surgery, please contact our office immediately at 248-662-1500. We may need to reschedule for your safety.

Step 3

When You Get Home

Recovering in the comfort of your own home is one of the advantages of outpatient surgery. Following your post-operative instructions carefully will support a smooth, speedy recovery.

A nurse will review your specific discharge instructions with you before you leave, and your surgeon's office will follow up with you after your procedure. The guidance below is general — always defer to your surgeon's specific instructions.

The First 24 Hours

  • Rest as much as possible — do not push through fatigue. Your body is healing.
  • Do not drive, operate machinery, or make major decisions while you are on pain medication or sedatives
  • Have your designated support person stay with you overnight if possible
  • Keep the surgical area elevated and apply ice as directed to minimize swelling
  • Take pain medication as prescribed — do not wait until pain becomes severe before taking it
  • Eat light foods and drink plenty of fluids to stay hydrated

Wound Care

  • Keep your incision clean and dry unless instructed otherwise
  • Follow your surgeon's specific dressing change instructions carefully
  • Do not submerge the wound in water (baths, pools, hot tubs) until cleared by your surgeon
  • Monitor the incision site for signs of infection: increasing redness, warmth, swelling, or discharge

When to Call or Seek Emergency Care

  • Fever above 101.5°F (38.6°C)
  • Severe or increasing pain not controlled by prescribed medication
  • Excessive bleeding, drainage, or wound opening
  • Chest pain, difficulty breathing, or calf pain and swelling (potential blood clot signs)
  • Nausea, vomiting, or inability to keep fluids down

Questions at home? Call us at 248-662-1500. For after-hours emergencies, please contact your surgeon's answering service or proceed to the nearest emergency room.
